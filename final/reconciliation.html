Monitor your performance, and calculate the impact

Monitoring pharmacy performance and calculating the impact of improved medication adherence is vital to the success of your pharmacy, now and in the future. Improving adherence not only improves patient care, but increases your script volume and reduces DIR fees.

Why adherence?

Here are some ways to monitor performance and calculate how improved adherence impacts your bottom line. Additionally, we’ve provided tools you can use to estimate, calculate and monitor DIR fees.

Tackle medication adherence

In order to improve medication adherence, pharmacies must focus on patient education coupled with workflow tools. Begin by assessing what patient education tools and techniques you are utilizing in your pharmacy and then review what workflow tools would work based on your patients’ barriers to adherence. Come up with your own action plan to improve adherence both with patient education and workflow management.

Why adherence

Medicare Star Ratings may not be new to you as these were created by CMS in 2006 as a way to track plan quality, with a number of measures directly impacted by pharmacy like yours.

So what does it mean for a contract to have performance criteria?

Contracting strategies vary among health plans/PBMs, but many are taking creative strategies to tie quality performance to reimbursements. And, while not all performance criteria are tied to Star Ratings, one commonality you will find in these criteria is the emphasis on the medication adherence quality measures asterisked above.

2018 Performance Criteria by PBM chart

Instead of a Medicare contract including straight reimbursement rates, many of the contracts now also include certain performance criteria a pharmacy must meet in order to avoid/reduce Direct and Indirect Remuneration (DIR) fees or receive a potential DIR rebate. Each year, we publish a Medicare 101 guide that details each major Medicare contract and its rates, performance criteria, DIR fee measurements and calculations, etc.
